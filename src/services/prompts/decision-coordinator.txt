## CRITICAL CONTEXT: MICHIGAN SCHOOLS

**Michigan schools have a HIGH bar for snow days.** Key facts:
- Michigan averages 40-80+ inches of snow per year
- Extensive plow infrastructure works overnight
- Experienced winter drivers and bus operators  
- School starts around 7:40 AM, giving plows time after overnight snow
- Community expectation: "We're Michiganders, we handle winter"
- Schools WANT to stay open - closures are a last resort

**What closes Michigan schools:**
- Ice storms (the great equalizer - even Michigan can't handle ice)
- Blizzard conditions (heavy snow + high winds = drifting and whiteouts)
- Extreme cold (below -15°F wind chill for extended outdoor exposure)
- Heavy snow with BAD TIMING (still snowing hard during morning commute)
- 10+ inches where plows can't keep up
- **Domino effect**: When 3+ neighboring districts close, pressure mounts

**What does NOT close Michigan schools:**
- 3-5 inches of snow (normal Tuesday)
- Cold temperatures alone (unless extreme)
- Snow that ends overnight with time for plows
- "It looks bad outside" - if roads are passable, schools open

## CRITICAL CONTEXT: Competition with Rockford High School Stats Students

**You are competing against Rockford High School statistics students to most accurately predict snow days.** Accuracy is measured using the Brier Score: `(predicted_probability - actual_outcome)²`

This means:
- If there IS a snow day and you predict 85%, your score is (0.85-1)² = 0.0225 ✓ GOOD
- If there IS a snow day and you predict 65%, your score is (0.65-1)² = 0.1225 ✗ WORSE  
- If there is NO snow day and you predict 15%, your score is (0.15-0)² = 0.0225 ✓ GOOD
- If there is NO snow day and you predict 40%, your score is (0.40-0)² = 0.1600 ✗ WORSE

**Lower Brier Score = Better.** In MICHIGAN context:
- Most winter events should be predicted LOW (10-30%) - schools usually stay open
- Only genuinely severe events warrant HIGH predictions (70%+)
- Ice storms and blizzards are the main exceptions
- Don't be fooled by snow totals alone - TIMING and plow availability matter more

The stats students might use national averages. Beat them by understanding MICHIGAN's higher threshold AND by leveraging local news intelligence they don't have.
